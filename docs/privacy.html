Privacy Policy — Memory App

Last updated: May 19, 2026

Overview

Memory is a personal diary app. Your privacy is fundamental to how it works — all your data stays on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information.

Data Collection

We collect nothing. Memory does not collect, transmit, or share any data with any server, third party, or developer.

Data Storage

All data you create in Memory — text entries, photos, voice memos, and emotional tone tags — is stored locally on your device using Android's internal storage. This data is:

  • Never uploaded to any server
  • Never shared with any third party
  • Never accessible to the developer
  • Only accessible to you, on your device

Permissions

Microphone (RECORD_AUDIO)
Used only to record voice memos within the app when you explicitly tap the record button. The app does not record audio in the background, does not access the microphone at any other time, and does not transmit audio recordings anywhere.

Photos

Photos are selected by you using Android's built-in photo picker. Selected photos are copied to the app's internal storage. They are never uploaded or shared.

Internet Access

Memory does not require an internet connection and does not make any network requests.

Data Deletion

You can delete individual memories within the app at any time. Uninstalling the app removes all app data from your device, unless Android's automatic backup is enabled in your device settings.

Children's Privacy

Memory does not collect any data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes in a future version, the updated date at the top of this page will reflect it.
